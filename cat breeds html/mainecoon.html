Maine Coon

Maine Coon

Feautres

  • Fluffy Fur Coat
  • Large Body and Weight
  • Pointy Ears with Long Fur at the End

Character Traits

  • Well-Tempered
  • Mellow
  • Relaxed

Origin

  • North America
  • Maine, U.S.A.

The Maine Coon is one of the largest cat breeds to be fully domesticated. Its most common features are its fluffy tail and long-haired coat. Though these traits can also be found in other cats, such as the Norwegian and Siberian Forest cats, their distinct traits involve their pointed ears (which only look pointy due to their fur), and very large size. In fact, they can get so big, they can grow to 4 feet (the average cat usually only makes it to 2)! Though most may see Maine Coons with a light brown or chocolate-colored coat, their fur patterns can look very different depending on the mix of breeds they are the offspring of. Fun fact: these cats are generally called: “The Gentle Giants,” not only due to their size, but their amazing temperament as well! Though any cat has the ability to get annoyed or aloof, Maine Coons are generally very calm and rarely attack other cats, as long as they don’t feel too threatened. Overall, these felines may be large, but they also have big hearts and can be very down-to-earth pets!
