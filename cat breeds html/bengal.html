Bengal

Bengal

Features

  • Spotted Fur Pattern
  • Muscular Body
  • Short Coat

Character Traits

  • Active
  • Chatty
  • Cuddly

Origin

  • Southeast Asia
  • U.S.A.

The Bengal cat is a domesticated cat breed created from a hybrid of the Asian leopard cat, with domestic cats, (mainly the spotted Egyptian Mau). Despite their wild background, these felines can be wonderful company with their sweet and active personalities. However, they are one of the most expensive cats to adopt! Their price tag can go up into the thousands, due to their exotic nature and high fostering costs. Though any cat can be socialized if found at a young-enough age, it may take more effort than others to socialize a Bengal cat, let alone if you can find one! However, if you can afford to adopt this breed from a trusted foster, they are very pleasant and sweet pets. Due to their muscular and sleek form, they need LOTS of activity and even be very chatty! In fact, Bengal cats have one of the loudest meows form a domesticated cat breed. So, if you do adopt, just make sure you're always attending to their needs!
