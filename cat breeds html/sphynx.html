Sphynx

Sphynx

Features

  • Hairless
  • Slim Body
  • Spotted Skib

Character Traits

  • Easy-Going
  • Friendly
  • Affectionate

Origin

  • Canada
  • U.S.A.

Anyone can spot a Sphynx cat from a mile away. Why? Well, they have no hair! They are a very identifiable breed due to their hairless form. Coming originally from Canada, their lack of hair is a result of a mutation found in certain cat litters. As they were bred, more and more Sphynx could be found all across America (though it’s more common to see Sphynx cats as indoor pets rather than outdoors.) They require human care most of the time due to their health, and may struggle out in the feral cat world. Even though they may look different than most cats, the Sphynx can still have the same level of affection as the Siamese or Burmese. They love to do anything and everything with their owners: Cuddling, getting attention, and having LOTS of play time. In fact, what makes the Sphynx so unique is their natural extroverted personalities that can blend well with almost any animal. So maybe, if you can get past the lack of fur and extra maintenance needed, Sphynx cats can be an amazing friend to you and your other pets!
