Burmese

Burmese

Features

  • Long Limbs
  • Brown, Short Coats
  • Muscular

Character Traits

  • Curious
  • Affectionate
  • Active

Origin

  • Asia
  • Thai-Burma

The Burmese is another wonderful cat breed, coming in from Asia like the Bengal ad Siamese. They’re believed to have come from Thai-Burma, as said in their name. Like the other breeds, they were eventually moved to America to continue the development of their breed. Overtime, they have developed very muscular and sleek bodies, but only partially as much as the Bengal or Siamese. Their fur coats tend to have more brown tints that very from their face and legs. As a side bonus for every pet owner: they tend to have thin coats, resulting in not as much shedding as a long-haired cat. They also share similar traits to the Siamese due to their affectionate and active personalities. This breed loves to play and explore anywhere they can go. They can also develop a strong attachment to their owners, due to their loyalty and adaptable nature as pets. They are quite vocal, and will always be sure to let you know when they do or don’t want to do something. Despite some skeletal issues that may have to be dealt with later on, the Burmese is still a wonderful pet to have for all ages!
