Tips for Cat Care

Pet Insurance and Vet Visits

Just like we can sometimes get sick, it's always important to give your cat frequent vet visits! Even if a cat appears healthy, there can always be something bad hiding in them! That's why getting pet insurance can help out in the long run for you and your pet! Learn more here!

Silence is Golden

Though there are many extroverted cats, most felines prefer quiet time and social distance. To prepare the best environment for your cat, give them plenty of hiding places and silent spots for them to rest or recharge.

You've Got a Friend In Me

Cats socialize best when they have a feline buddy with them. If you have the money and space, try to adopt at least two cats so they can have a friend to play and cuddle with while you’re away!

Lights, Toys, Action!

Just like people, cats need daily exercise and playtime. They are natural hunters and need things to prey on. Make sure you have plenty of toys and climbing spaces that’ll give your cat the necessary stimulation they need!

The Top of the World

Though cats tend to sleep anywhere they find comfortable, the best place for cats to sleep is either on high platforms or hard to reach places. If you’re looking for a place to put a cat bed, a sturdy shelf or cat tree can be a purrfect place!

Litter Box

What makes cats a special pet is their use of litter boxes. If for any reason, a cat stops using its litter box, that’s usually a sign that the litter needs to be changed, or the cat needs more than one area to “do their business.”

Strong Smells

A cat’s sense of smell is 14x stronger than humans (which is why cats always sniff as a greeting). With such strong sensors, cats prefer not to be around strong or odorous smells. If you don’t want a cat in a room or on a surface, a good tip is to spray it down with a citrus odor. Cats will avoid that area like the plague!

DO NOT DECLAW YOUR CAT

.When a cat is declawed, the tendon is removed : The equivalent of cutting the tip of a person’s finger to prevent nails from growing.This could cause pain and lameness later on in the cat's life. If you’re having trouble with your cat scratching everything, consider buying a decent pair of claw clippers and trim the tip of your cat’s claws. It doesn’t hurt the cat, and their dexterity will remain in-tact, and importantly, they won’t be in pain!

Scratch Scratch Scratch

Going off of cat claws, make sure to buy plenty of scratching boards (or one nice, sturdy post) for your cat. A feline needs to stretch and sharpen their claws, and unless you enjoy torn-up furniture and clothes, make sure to invest in a good scratching area.

The Scratch of Death

Though cats are generally clean creatures (at least on the outside), always try to avoid being scratched or bitten by one. They can sometimes carry harmful germs that, while normal to them, are toxic and unhealthy to humans. Cat scratch fever is a common result of cat scratches and can even be deadly depending on the severity.

Breathe it In!

If you plan to own a cat that’s purely indoors, make sure your home or apartment has plenty of windows and access to fresh air. Even if a cat is indoors, they’ll always enjoy looking outside and breathing in the lovely smells of nature! They may also participate in bird watching and sunbathing.

Shy, but Still Sweet

Though most cats are shy and more introverted creatures, they’ll always enjoy a good scratch and pet here and there. Make sure to give your cat plenty of love and affection so they can know how much you appreciate them!
